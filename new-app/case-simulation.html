History of Present Illness

Patient is a 45-year-old male who presents to the emergency department with a 1-hour history of severe, crushing chest pain. The pain is located in the center of his chest and radiates to his left arm and jaw. He describes the pain as pressure-like and rates it as 8/10 in severity. The pain started while he was watching TV and has been constant since onset. He reports associated shortness of breath, nausea, and diaphoresis. He denies fever, cough, or recent trauma.

Past Medical History

  • Hypertension (diagnosed 5 years ago)
  • Hyperlipidemia (diagnosed 3 years ago)
  • Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (diagnosed 2 years ago)

Medications

  • Lisinopril 10mg daily
  • Atorvastatin 20mg daily
  • Metformin 500mg twice daily

Social History

Patient is a current smoker with a 20 pack-year history. He drinks alcohol socially (2-3 beers per week) and denies illicit drug use. He works as an accountant and reports a high-stress job. He is married with two children.

Family History

Father had a myocardial infarction at age 50. Mother has hypertension and type 2 diabetes. No family history of sudden cardiac death.

AI Recommendation

Based on the patient's presentation and findings, the most likely diagnosis is Acute Myocardial Infarction (STEMI).

Confidence: 90%

Key findings:

  • Crushing chest pain radiating to left arm and jaw
  • ST-segment elevation on ECG
  • Elevated cardiac enzymes (Troponin I, CK-MB)
  • Risk factors: hypertension, hyperlipidemia, diabetes, smoking

Case Notes

This is a classic presentation of an acute myocardial infarction (heart attack). The patient has multiple risk factors and presents with typical symptoms and diagnostic findings.

Remember to consider the following in your diagnosis:
